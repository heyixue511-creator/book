# THE SENSE OF ORDER A Study in the Psychology of Decorative Art E. H. GOMBRICH

"Gombrich's Sense of Order is a peer for his Art and Illusion, as wise and as witty, as revealing and wide-ranging. His explorations of the realm of design are splendid adventures, presented triumphantly. He is candid about his own ideas as well as those of others, and his magnificent illustrations call for our attention as much as his commentary calls for our serious thought."

Journal of Aesthetics and Art Criticism

"We are fortunate in having E. H. Gombrich to pursue the sometimes elusive relations between art and psychology. The Sense of Order once again proves that Mr. Gombrich's work is valuable. Not only is his knowledge of both art history and psychology almost encyclopedic, but he approaches his subject with a pragmatic point of view, explaining everything in the simplest terms, pointing out where we simply have no data, appropriating models from other fields when he finds them useful, and reviewing clearly what we do know.'

Antiques World

"Gombrich has attempted nothing less than a global analysis of the entire range of the decorative arts, from prehistoric epochs to the present. . . . [He] has made a brilliant and original contribution. . . . The Sense of Order is enhanced with color plates, black and white illustrations and photographs which explicate the text ànd clarify the argument. It will no doubt be as widely read and discussed as Art and Illusion."

Portfolio

"Gombrich stimulates and entertains. His own volume is an imposition of high order on the profusion of art books that offer a thousand views but not a single vision."

Time

"Mr. Gombrich is no run-of-the-mill art historian. Every feather sticks to his magnet. To read him is to be carried along by a euphoric mix of propositions ransacked from biology, psychology, semiotics, information theory, art. . . . Who else can impart such excitement to the zoomorphic shapes on a Chinese coffin handle or the symmetrical pattern of overlapping rosettes that turns up in Phoenician ivories and Assyrian pavements?"

Suzi Gablik, The New Tork Times Book Review

E. H. Gombrich was Director of the Warburg Institute and Professor of the History of the Classical Tradition in the University of London until his retirement in 1976.
