# Abstract

This study traces the design thinking of Victor Papanek in the historical context of the ethical dimension of modern design. It revolves around a potential question: how should modern design, a complex formation based on the knowledge, experience and creativity accumulated and grasped by designers since the 19th century, be applied and developed in our time to serve the best interests of human beings?

The study first reviews the ethical concern of design pioneers in Europe from the pre WWlI period, how their professional awareness and ethics emerged from a context of rising consumer design discourse in America. Experiences from this period basically foregrounded the historical framework for ethical discussions surrounding the discipline. The study then goes on to trace the effect of various design discourses in the post-war period, how the historical conditions of the 60s and the tradition of organic modern design influenced Victor Papanek's working on his magnum opus Design for the Real World by the late 1960s. This chapter contains a research on the immediate background of Victor Papanek's design thinking and constitutes a historical cross-section in our understanding of the thiker. hapter I,the core ths sudy, mainy focuses n Victr Papanes basindersan and propositions on design, the various visions and solutions he points to. Examining through the logical structure of his design thinking, the study tries to reveal both its rationale and limits. Chapter V discusses the evolvement of design ethics in both the theoretical and practical dimension since the 1990s, from which we can tell that meeting up all Papanek's calls is a rather systematic undertaking that involves the participation of multiple parties and fields including enterprises, NGOs, governments as well as legislative bodies.
