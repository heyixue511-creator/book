## Chapter 5 : Calligraphy and painting

Storing paintings: Paintings should be stored in caskets of fir wood , with absolutely no use of lacquer or glued paper linings internally, lest they seep out and stain the picture . In the fourth or fifth month, you should unroll every piece and give them a brief sight of the sun , then return them to their boxes at a distance of ten or so feet from g round level , to pre ent foxing. Generally, when displaying pictures , they should be changed every three to five days, to prevent both fatiguing the eye and damage by dust. When taking them down, first whisk the dust o ff both sides o f a scroll, so that the surface will remain undamaged .5
