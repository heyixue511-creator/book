## Henry Cole's principal pronouncements

Cole's utterances were essentially occasional, linked to whatever causes he was fighting for, and are sometimes hard to track down, He reprinted a selection of pieces that he thought significant in volume 2 of Fifty Years. The following list, a somewhat different selection from Cole's own and biased towards art and industry, represents the authors' choice of pieces that most vividly express his opinions.

ElIZAbeTH BonytHoN is a nineteenth-century specialist whose connection with the V&A began with the Victorian Church Art exhibition in 197o-71. She is now an Honorary Fellow of the V&A Research Department. She has produced a typed transcript and index of the fifty-seven volumes of Cole's diaries, which means that she has a more intimate acquaintance with Cole than any other contemporary scholar. She has written articles on Cole, and the V&A published her King Cole: A picture portrait of Sir Henry Cole in 1982.

AnthonY Burton worked at the V&A in several capacities, including as Assistant Keeper of the National Art Library (1968-79), and Head of the Bethnal Green Museum of Childhood (1981-97). He worked on over a hundred exhibitions including Victorian Church Art. Until retirement in 2002 he was Senior Research Fellow in Museology and Museum History in the V&A Research Department. In 1999 he published Vision  Accident: The Story of the Victoria and Albert Museum, the first comprehensive history of the V&A's politics and personalities.
