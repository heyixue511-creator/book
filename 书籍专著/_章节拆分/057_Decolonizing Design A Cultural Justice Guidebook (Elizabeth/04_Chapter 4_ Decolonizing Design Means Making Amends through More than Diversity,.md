## Chapter 4: Decolonizing Design Means Making Amends through More than Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

Dori opens chapter four with her experience of being bullied out of a job. Design firms and institutions see diversity, equity, and inclusion [DEI] hiring initiatives as ways to make amends for their neglect and exclusion of diverse communities and identities. In the summer of 2020 during the aftermath of the murder of George Floyd, there was a rush of firms and institutions to make amends. These initiatives are often great steps toward social and economic justice, but they are not enough without a real change in power relations by redistributing power to those communities and identities that have been structurally excluded, which is how we decolonize design. These initiatives need to avoid the hiring pitfalls of seeking a supertoken employee or forcing the cultural assimilation of diverse employees to achieve success. In this chapter, Dori shares the challenges as well as helpful tips of OCAD U's successful Black cluster hire, to show how you might find success in your hiring initiatives by calling out to the interests of diverse communities, changing the criteria of evaluation to take systemic exclusion into account, and thereby building a critical mass of diverse individuals.
