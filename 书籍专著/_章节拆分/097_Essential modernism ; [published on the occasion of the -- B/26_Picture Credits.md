The importance of the Bauhaus, the seminal German art and design institute, is also addressed, along with the spread of Modernism from its origins in Europe to the United States and the emigration of European Modernists to America in the late 1930s and early 1940s.

Illustrated with almost 100 images from the exhibition, Essential Modernism tells the story of how artists reacted to the unprecedented violence and destruction they had witnessed during World War I; and their experimental search for ways to create a better world through art.

Philip Brookman is Director of Curatorial Affairs at the Corcoran Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C.

Paul Greenhalgh is Director and President of the Corcoran Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C.

Sarah Newman is Assistant Curator of Contemporary Art at the Corcoran Gallery of Art, Washington, D.C.
