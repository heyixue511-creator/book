## ESSENTIAL MODERNISM

Never before have the conditions of life changed so swiftly and enormously as they have changed for mankind in the last fifty years. We have been carried along ... [and] we are only now beginning to realize the force and strength of the storm of change that has come upon us. — H.G. WELLS

When British visionary author H.G.Wells wrote this passge in 1933 he knew he was living in a time of absolute transformation. Essential Modernism chronicles how artists, responding to the violence of World War I and the unprecedented modernization of the world, tried to change society.

Written as a guide to the Corcoran Gallery of Art's presentation of the groundbreaking exhibition Modernism: Designing a New World 1914-1939, Essential Modernism brings into focus the foundation and meaning of Modernism— the defining movement of twentieth-century art, design, and culture.It clarifies many of the ideas and principies underpinning the aesthetic and visual appearance of much art, architecture, graphic and product design, film, and theater desig createdin the frsthalofthetwentieth century.

Essential Modernism traces the historic development of modern form, starting with the post-World War I philosophical and artistic search for Utopia—a new and improved world free from conflict, greed, and inequality. It investigates the role of the factory and mass production in modernization, as well as the social and political agendas of different art movements during the interwar years.

Surveying important objects and images from many media and industries involved in shaping the twentieth century, this book includes sections on early Cubism, Futurism, De Stijl, Purism, Suprematism, Constructivism, and Precisionism, among other movements.
