## Modernism Spreads through America

After World War I, industrial mechanization and commercial expansion dramatically changed the American landscape. Massive skyscrapers and factories grew and multiplied, dominating a seemingly once-limitless horizon.

As in Europe, this new environment produced a strong effect on the arts. The clean and precise forms of the machine led some American artists, photographers, and designers to champion themes of industrialization, technology, and innovation.
