## Kandinsky The Language of the Eye Paul Overy

The dramatic nature of Kandinsky's rejection of representation in painting has obscuredother equally important aspects of his originality: in this book, the circumstances that led Kandinsky to produce the first viable abstract paintings are carefully considered, but the main emphasis is laid on the highly original and consciously constructed works that Kandinsky produced later in his life at the Bauhaus and in Paris.

Paul Overy describes Kandinsky's early work, and then geson oiscuss h relationshiphe ntepy European art movements, up to his death in 1944. The main body of the book is devoted to a discussion of those of Kandinsky's paintings which developed out of his novel ideas on composition and communication, and the author draws on his sometimes difficult writings to illuminate their meaning. His book is an important revaluation of the work of an artist who has profoundly influenced twentieth-century painting.

## New Tendencies in Art Aldo Pellegrini

Aldo Pellegrini examines the work of contemporary artists, and the movements of art that have developed since World War II. He shows how Futurism, Cubisn, Fauvism, and Expressionism paved the way for a break with the past, and how Dadaism and Surrealism were a bridge to the present. In doing so he provides a basic understanding of Pop Art, New Abstraction, Hard-Edge, Op Art, New Tendency, Programmed Art and Happenings.

'A guided tour through the jungle of modern art, and an original, intelligent and exceptionally well-illustrated one.' Observer

'A lucidly written text, chronological surveys and biographical notes with three hundred illustrations, many in colour, make this book a useful addition to any library.' Arts Review
