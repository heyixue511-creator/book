Designers are notoriously visually aware and sensitive people. They are usually interested in art, have a heightened sense of dress and fashion, like the iconoclastic and appear different themselves. Designers, however, clearly use another form of cognition which is generally describe s visualthinking' Theythink diretly by manipulating graphical information which is extremely difficult to encode entirely in conventional symbolie systems as required by AI. This is yet another reason why it sees unlikely that computers can ever be enabled to design in the sense that humans do. The problem that faces the designer is the interface between what we might call perception and imagery. The psychology of perception deals with the way we see and make sense of a world around us. This would include the way we look at drawings. In relying on precedent the designer deals with a second kind of material that is mentally imaged and not physically present. A third even more challenging kind of material is the imagination the designer uses to conjure up designs that have never previously existed. A large part of design thinking then must rely upon relating these three kinds of information.
