## 81 Alec Issigonis

Austin Mini (1959) as seen in the Paramount Picture Corporation's film The Italian Job, 1969 Following the Suez crisis of 1956, the British Motor Corporation decided to design a small, economical family car, resulting in the production of the Mini in 1959. Its innovations included the space-saving transverse engine which provided a capacious interor in relation to its external dimensions. It was not particularly successful when launched but, following successes in the Monte Carlo Rally of 1964 and 1965, its sales passed one million in 96. Its attractive price ensured widespread appeal, and it became a symbol both British Pop and urban chic.
