## 5 Discussion

Our protocol analysis method is based on the topical viewpoint. However, the analysis is still not complete, because only verbal utterances are taken into account while gestures and movements are not analysed. Moreover, the hypothesis on the anticipatory approach must be demonstrated through a thorough analysis.

The originality of the theory of this study is due to the focus on argumentation in design. The distinguishing features of this work can be indicated as follows:

Firstly, there are very few studies on the subject chosen for our study.

Secondly, our protocol analysis method is in our opinion the first one focusing on the role of argumentation during the problem solving activity.

Our method addresses both individual and collective activities. It is important to realize that in general there are only a few protocol analysis studies performed on a team.

This study has partially confirmed the theoretical issues on two topics:

1. The problem solving process can be charactarized by the location of the discursive space used while the importance of the designer's discursive skill is obvious.

2. The approach by designers during their design process can be characterized as an anticipatory process.

Finally, we think that considering design as a discursive activity and extending protocol analyis methods with a semio-linguistic viewpoint are very important in order to have a better understanding of the complexity of the design activity and mainly of the different viewpoints of the designers used during the design process. We think that these results have some significant theoretical implications on 'Design Research' and on 'Design Rationale'3. Indeed we can characterize the discursive space used by each agent linked to each sub-step, i.e. according to our topical viewpoint we can define different types of problem solving rationales which occur in the design process.

Cyclic process by the following sub-steps. This step concerns both the conceptual and detailed design. In conceptual design some steps are related to the device (3.1, 3.3, 3.4, 3.5, 3.6, 3.8) and the others to the interface aspects (3.2, 3.7, 3.9):

3.1 Location (00:26-00:35): Looking first at the location on the bike, evaluating by anticipating relevant elements (joining techniques, materials, marketing, ease of use, analogous products). No selection yet.

3.2 Joining techniques (00:35-00:50): (Pack to rack, rack to bike) General discussion, no selection, evaluation on centre of gravity and ergonomic aspects. Implicit choice for rear location.

3.3 Device (00:50-01:04): Several ideas, information on Blackburn racks, trailer idea and evaluation (costs).

3.4 Materials (01:04-01:07): Trying to come up with ideas through materials discussion; short period, no decisions.

3.5 Device (01:07-01:15): Generating ideas. Evaluation by way of first cost estimates for device.

3.6 Device (01:15-01:19):

Decision on location, horizontal position pack.
