Their conservatism was equalled, even exceeded, by that of the manufacturers of the new typesetting machines, whose engineering innovations were not matched by any corresponding audacity in design for many years. We must remember that Monotype and Linotype were not selling type design, or even type, but composing machines, and the temptation was understandably to equip them with faces that looked as like the foundry type they were replacing as possible.
