Sebastian Carter was born in 1941, and educated at King's College, Cambridge, where he read English, Architecture and Fine Arts. After university he joined the London publisher John Murray, and in 1963 went to Paris to work for the Trianon Press, who produced the William Blake Trust facsimiles. After two years, he returned to London and divided his time between Ruari McLean Associates, The Stellar Press and freelance commissions, before joining his father Will Carter at the Rampant Lions Press in Cambridge. Here they concentrate on the design and printing of ephemera and limited editions, notably illustrated books in collaboration with Michael Rothenstein, the late Anthony Gross, and many other artists. In 1982 their work was the subject of a major retrospective exhibition at the Fitzwilliam Museum, Cambridge.

In 1971, Sebastian Carter won the first Francis Minns Award, given by the National Book League to a designer singled out by the judges at the annual British book design and production exhibition. His essay First principles of typography in the 197os was published by Bowaters in 1973, and his The book becomes: the making of a fine edition appeared in 1984; he has also contributed to Bogvennen, The Times literary supplement, Crafts and Matrix. In 19834 he was President of the Double Crown Club.
