## Broom

Nineteenth century

C'ommunity unknown

Broom corn, pine handle and crossbar. cotton twine, oak or hickory brace. leather. iron tacks and hardware 671 × 24 × 1 gin. (including handle) The Shaker Museum. Old Chatham. New York

The Shakers in several communities made brooms and brushes for home use and for sale. This example is unusually wide ; perhaps it was intended for the wide floors of a meeting room or long dwelling-house hall.

Theodore Bates (17621846). of Watervliet, New York. ran a broom-making operation for many years. The Shakers later credited him with development of the now-standard flat broom. c.1soo, an improvement over the traditional round broom.
