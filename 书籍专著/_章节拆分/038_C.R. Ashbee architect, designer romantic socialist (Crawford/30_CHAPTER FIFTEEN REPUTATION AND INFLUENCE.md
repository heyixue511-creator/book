I have come across some examples of Ashbee's stylistic influence in Britain, in several media, and there are no doubt many others. But he does not seem to have been imitated as obviously or as often as Beardsley for instance, or Voysey or Baillie Scott. The earliest and most obvious example is that the motif of a spreading tree used in Transactions, for instance on p. 23, was taken up and closely copied by Laurence Housman in his first success as an illustrator, George Meredith's Jump to Glory Jane (1892). There is no evidence that Housman knew Ashbee at this date.
