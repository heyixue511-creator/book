## Critiques of the Posthuman

This article would not be complete without a discussion of an important critique of the posthuman from critical race studies. It relates to the notion that design, architecture, and related fields have incorporated an understanding of the human based on the notion of a universal subject—usually white, male, privileged, well-off, and young—that does not exist in reality. For example, Michelle Murphy’s work on sick building syndrome (SBS) gives an account of a literal “Man in a Box” that was used to test early air conditioning systems.108 She writes, “Inside the box, young white men, mostly engineering students, sometimes stripped to underwear, repetitively lifted light weights. Outside the chamber, the researchers, sleeves rolled, used sensitive instruments such as the hot-wire anemometer and the whirled psychrometer to monitor the interior atmosphere.” 109
