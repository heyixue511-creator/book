## Building a tower.

Fig. 51: Stone I rests on the foundation stone. This upsets the balance toward left. To equalize, and causing a new disturbance, stone II is added to the right. Fol lowing this pattern, stone III pulls toward left, stone IV equalizes and pulls toward right, etc., until finally the keystone establishes a definitive equilibrium.

Fig. 52: P1, P2, P3, etc., are the pivot points of the toppling stones. Connected with each other by a line, they form a zig-zag pattern that circumscribes the ver tical axis.

## EARTH, WATER AND AIR.

Symbols of the static area are plummet (position) and balance. The plummet aims at the earth center where all materially-bound existence is anchored.

But there are regions with different laws and new symbols, signifying freer movement and dynamic position.

Water and atmosphere are transitional regions.
