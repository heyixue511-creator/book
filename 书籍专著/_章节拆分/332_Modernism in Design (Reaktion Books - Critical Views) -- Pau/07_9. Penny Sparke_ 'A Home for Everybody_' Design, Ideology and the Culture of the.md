# Modernism in Design edited by Paul Greenhalgh

with essays by Tim Benton, Gillian Naylor, Penny Sparke, Wendy Kaplan, Clive Wainwright, Martin Gaughan, Guy Julier, Mimi Wils, Julian Holder and Paul Greehalgh.

The Modern Movement has dominated debate in design for over sixty years. During the last two decades it has become the focus of intense discussion as designers, critics and historians attempt to reposition it in relation to contemporary culture. Unfortunately, the anger the argument has generated often reduces Modernism to the level of caricature.

This book moves away from defamatory or idealistic positions in an attempt to reconstruct Modernism as an historical phenomenon. These new and important essays cover Modernism's fortunes in Germany, Italy, Sweden, Britain, Spain, Belgium and the USA; they range in subject-matter from the World's Fairs and everyday domestic objects to American West Coast architecture and French and Italian furniture.

Modernism in Design is a thought-provoking examination of a number of theoretical and practical issues, all of which are crucial in any discussion of Modernist design, regardless of whether we are for or against it.
