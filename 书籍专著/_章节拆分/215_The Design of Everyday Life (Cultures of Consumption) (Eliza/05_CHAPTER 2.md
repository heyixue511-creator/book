## CHAPTER 2

1. In each household we identified and interviewed one ‘primary’ respondent, including other members of the household if they wanted to join in. Fifteen of the forty households were couples living with dependent children, seventeen were couples without dependent children at home, and eight were single-person households. Five interviews were conducted with men, nineteen with women and sixteen with couples. Women are over-represented in the sample, although when including couples, twenty-one men were interviewed. All respondents were white and ages ranged from twenty-four to eighty years; no age group was disproportionately represented in the sample or in each household type.
