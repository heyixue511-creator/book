## 6 Nippon

(934-44)

Natori Yonosuke, on the revival of his studio, Nippon Kb, in 1934, reportedly asked the founder of Japanese art direction, ta Hideshige, who would be the best young designers to help found a new multilingual periodical titled Nippon to introduce Japanese culture into English-, French-, German-, and Spanish-speaking regions. Öta responded: Kno Takashi and Yamana Ayao. Nippon, more than any other national promotional periodical from the belligerent powers of the 1930s, attempted a total integration of design. From the first issue to the last, no Japanese ideographs appeared on the covers—not for image reasons but because Nippon provided a typographic statement for this team of designers who viewed existing Japanese typography as the greatest impediment to the development of a "new Japanese graphic design and style." Thus Nippon and the Hara Hiromu-designed Front (published 194144 as a war propaganda picture magazine) used Roman letter forms. Illustrations shown, all designed by Kno Takashi, are:
