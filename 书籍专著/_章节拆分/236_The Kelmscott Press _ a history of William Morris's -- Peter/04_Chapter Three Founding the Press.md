55. The present locations of three of Morris's four presses are known. (a) The small proofing press, unlike the other three, was not acquired by C. R. Ashbee; it was advertised for sale at 3o guineas by Tregaskis in 1898, apparently without success, for it remained in the basement of the Tregaskis Bookshop in London for a number of years. In the 192os Dr John W. Bartlett of New York bought it. Theodore M. Lilienthal purchased it from Bartlett in 1939; and it is now in the Huntington Library. (See Lilienthal, A William Morris Press goes West (Berkeley, Calif., 1961).) (b) Either the first or second of Morris's presses passed from Ashbee in 1913 and through several intermediate owners to the Shakespeare Head Press; Sir Basil Blackwell gave it to the William Morris Society, and it is at present, appropriately, in the basement of Kelmscott House.
