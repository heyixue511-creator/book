## 2 There is no infallibly correct process

Much though some early writers on design methodology may have wished it, there is no infallibly good way of designing. In design the solution is not just the logical outcome of the problem, and there is therefore no sequence of operations which will guarantee a result. The situation, however, is not quite as hopeless as this statement may suggest. We saw in chapter 6 how it is possible to analyse the structure of design problems and in the next section we shall explore the way designers can and do modify their process in response to this variable problem structure. In fact we shall see how controlling and varying the design process is one of the most important skills a designer must develop.
