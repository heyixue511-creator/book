## Kenneth Frampton

was born in 1930 and trained as an architect at the Architectural Association School of Architecture, London. He has worked as an architect and as an architectural historian and critic, and is now Ware Professor at the Graduate School of Architecture and Planning, Columbia University, New York. He has taught at a number of leading institutions in the field, including the Royal College of Art in London, the ETH in Zurich, the Berlage Institute in Amsterdam and, most recently, the EPFL in Lausanne. He is the author of numerous essays on modern and contemporary architecture, and served from 1986 to r99o as president of the jury for the EEC European Prize in Architecture, given under the auspices of the Mies van der Rohe Foundation in Barcelona. His most recent theoretical work, Studies inTectonic Culture, was published at the end of 1995.
