# CHAPTER III.

ON THE PRINCIPLE OF CONTRAST OF COLOURS IN ITS CONNECTION WITH THE FABRICATION OF BAVONNERIE CARPETS.

(406.) Ir it be true that a knowledge of the principle of contrast is less neceesary to the worker of Savonnerie than it is to him of the Gobelins, yet it would be & mistake to suppose that the first can remain ignorant of it without disadvantage.

In fact, although the worker of Savonnerie need not so assiduously copy his model faithfully in its colouring as the Gobelins weaver, and as the five or sir differently coloured threads which he can unite to form a compound thread may be very favourable to the gradation, and to the passage of one colour into another, yet this same liberty which permits of a slight departure from the colours of the model, imposes the obligation of making him produce the best possible effect.

Can be follow a better guide than the law of contrast when he seeks to attain this aim?
