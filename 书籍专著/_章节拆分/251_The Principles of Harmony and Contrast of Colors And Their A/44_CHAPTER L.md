# CHAPTER L

## ON THE COMPARISON OF TWO PATTERNS OF THE SAME COLOCR

(836.) When it is a question of two patterns of any kind, which relate to the same colour, be it blue or red, if there is no identity between the tints of the two patterns when compared together, we must take into account the contrast which eraggerates the difference : thus, if the one be greenish blue, it will make the other appear less green or more indigo, or even more violet than it really is; and, reciprocally, the first will appear greoner than when viewed isolated; the same with the reds, if one is more orange than the other, this latter will appear more purple and the former more orange than they really are.
